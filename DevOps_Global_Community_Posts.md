# DevOps.global — Community Post Pack

> Ready-to-paste posts and DMs for the channels you have today: **Andela community**, **Slack communities**, and **Discord communities**. Plus a candidate-facing one-pager and bio templates for your profiles.

---

## How to use this pack

1. Pick the post that matches the community's tone (technical-formal, casual-builder, regional-supportive, etc.).
2. Replace the `<<< … >>>` placeholders.
3. Always include a single, clear call-to-action — either your intake form URL or a DM ask.
4. Log every post and reply into the Outreach Log of your Tracker. Use channel = "Slack community", "Discord community", or "Andela community" so the Channels tab tells you what's working.

Cadence rule of thumb: **engage before you ask**. In any new community, reply to 2–3 technical conversations before you post your first opportunity. Recruiters who only post jobs get muted; recruiters who participate get DMs.

## 12. The two posts you should NOT make

A premium agency loses credibility in 24 hours with the wrong post. Avoid:

**❌ "Hiring DevOps Engineer at $client! Apply now."**
We are not allowed to name clients or quote salaries. Replace with the structured agency intros above.

**❌ "Anyone interested in DevOps roles, DM me your CV."**
Spray-and-pray destroys reply rates and trust. Replace with engagement first, then a structured post with a clear intake link.

## 13. Posting schedule — keep yourself honest

| Frequency | What you post |
|---|---|
| Daily, in every active community | 1–2 helpful replies in technical threads. Build presence. |
| 1× per week per community | 1 structured opportunity / intro post (rotate which template you use). |
| 1× per fortnight, top community | 1 engagement / re-engagement post if and only if you've been active there. |
| Never | Mass-DMing a list. Reposting the same opportunity in the same channel within 7 days. Naming clients. Quoting salaries. |
