## 2. Profile picture frame

LinkedIn has a built-in "Hiring" frame that adds a green badge around your photo and signals to the LinkedIn algorithm that you're actively recruiting.

1. Click your profile picture ‚Üí **Frames**.
2. Select **Hiring**.
3. Save.

This single change unlocks LinkedIn-specific recruiter behaviour: people searching with "is hiring" filters can find you, and your profile gets the small green "Hiring" badge in feeds and search results.

## 9. Engagement playbook for outbound

Once your profile is set up, the active sourcing side runs in parallel:

- Use LinkedIn search for keywords matching your role list ‚ÄĒ e.g. `"Kubernetes" "Terraform" remote` filtered to your target geographies.
- For each strong-looking profile, send the **¬ß5.2 cold DM template** from the Recruiter Playbook (paste a personalised first line referencing their actual work).
- Log every DM in the Outreach Log of your Excel Tracker with channel = "LinkedIn (organic)" so the Channels tab can show you what's converting.

Once Kerstin enables LinkedIn Recruiter (from Week 3 onwards per your work instructions), this scales considerably ‚ÄĒ but the organic profile + posting rhythm above works even without it.

## 10. What to NOT do on LinkedIn

- Don't post the same role-specific post twice within 4 weeks. LinkedIn's algorithm de-prioritises repetition.
- Don't tag specific clients or quote salaries ‚ÄĒ violates Kerstin's rules.
- Don't promise interviews, placements, or specific outcomes.
- Don't mass-DM with the same copy ‚ÄĒ LinkedIn detects this and throttles your account.
- Don't connect-spam ‚ÄĒ sending 100 connect requests/day will get your account flagged. ~20/day, all with a personalised note, is the safe upper bound.
- Don't post "Hiring!" without specifics ‚ÄĒ generic posts get scrolled past. Each post should name specific roles, specific tech, and a specific call-to-action.
